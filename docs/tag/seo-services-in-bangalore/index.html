How to use landing pages for your business-2018?

When you are planning to create a Google AdWords for your business. The landing page is your key chance to convince the customer to convert – to buy your products, fill out a form or download a resource. You should have high-quality landing pages to optimize conversion rates, and highly relevant pages will also lower your conversion costs.

Very important quality of an optimized landing page is relevance: Your ad text should be relevant to your keywords, your landing page should be relevant to your Google ad. Landing page with relevant ad text also gets higher quality scores and better ad rankings as a result.

Designing specific landing pages that talk directly to the user’s search query will have a real impact on your AdWords account and, most importantly, drive more conversions. It’s worth it to devote major time and effort to optimize your AdWords landing pages.

Create Optimized Landing Pages for Google AdWords

Creating AdWords landing pages can be a hard task – there’s a lot to take into consideration. Some challenges of creating AdWords landing pages include:

You can’t build landing pages in AdWords:

You can’t have great AdWords ads for your business without solid landing pages, Google won’t provide landing page assistance within AdWords. So we need to build landing pages separately.

You need outside help to make landing page:

Designing landing pages generally choose the best web design company to edit and manage the pages, that web designer to design the pages and a hosting environment. That means major time and money.

Specific requirements for landing pages:

The good landing page has many different elements to consider – your landing pages need to be relevant to your keyword and ads in order to earn a good Quality Score. You also need to have conversion tracking properly set up to track what is and is not working.

Follow the steps to improve your landing page experience:

1. It should be offer relevant, genuine content and Helpful

  • Your landing page should contain direct relevant to your google ads text and keywords.
  • Provide specific thing while someone clicks on ads. if someone clicks for a particular bike model, they shouldn’t get all the bikes on the landing page.
  • Provide options when a user wants options. if someone’s looking to compare cars, they probably don’t want to go specific model page. They expect more.
  • Give a useful information about whatever you are advertising

2. Increase transparency and loyalty on your website

  • Give proper information about your business and what your business does
  • Share everything about your product or services before asking visitors to fill out forms
  • Show your contact details to find your visitors easily
  • Before asking the personal details of the customers, make it clear why you are asking and what you going to do with it

3. Create mobile and computer navigation easy

  • Create and design your page well, so people don’t have to hunt around for information.
  • Create the best ads that make the peoples order your product.
  • when the customers in your landing page don’t annoy with pop-ups or other features that interfere with their navigation of your site.
  • Help the customers to find what they are really expected.

4. Try to Decrease your landing page loading time

  • Make it sure your landing page loads quickly once someone clicks on your ad, whether on a computer or mobile device.
  • Consider changing your landing page into an Accelerated Mobile Page (AMP).
  • Check your site scores on mobile speed, and get quick fixes to improve it.

The Benefits of Using Video on Landing Pages

whenever possible you can use the video on your landing pages. It provides a passive engagement medium where visitors can experience your message with very little effort. If you are using video on landing pages can increase conversion by 80%.

Some reasons why video can lift your landing page conversion rates

  • Landing page’s video increase the length of time and people stay on your landing page for more time, giving your brand message longer to sink in.
  • If you feature yourself or company employees in the video, the trust factor is raised significantly.
  • Customers are lazy and prefer to watch vs. read.
